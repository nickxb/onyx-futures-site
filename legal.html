5. Evaluation Rules & Account Conduct

To pass an ONYX evaluation and qualify for a simulated funded (Diamond) account, you must meet all of the following conditions:

  • Profit Target: Reach the required profit target for your account size (see plan details).
  • Minimum Trading Days: Complete a minimum of 3 trading days before the evaluation can be passed.
  • End-of-Day Drawdown: Never exceed the maximum EOD trailing drawdown. Drawdown is calculated at market close — not intraday — giving you room to recover during the session.
  • No Consistency Rule: There is no consistency rule during evaluation. One big green day counts. Trade your natural style.
  • No Daily Loss Limit: There is no daily loss limit during evaluation. Your only hard limit is the EOD trailing drawdown.
  • Contract Limits: You may not exceed the maximum contract limit for your account size at any time.
  • Allowed Instruments: Futures contracts listed on CME, COMEX, NYMEX, and CBOT only. Stocks, options, forex, crypto, and CFDs are strictly prohibited.
  • News Trading: You must exit all positions at least 2 minutes before scheduled major economic releases (FOMC, NFP, CPI, etc.).
  • Maximum Accounts: Traders may hold up to 10 active accounts (evaluation or funded) simultaneously.
  • No Prohibited Strategies: Latency arbitrage, account copying between accounts, and exploitation of system vulnerabilities are prohibited and will result in immediate disqualification.

6. Funded (Diamond) Accounts

Upon passing an evaluation, you may be offered a simulated Diamond account. Diamond accounts operate under the following terms:

  • Profit split: 80% to the trader, 20% to ONYX.
  • Payouts are processed weekly upon request, subject to verification.
  • Consistency Rule applies on funded accounts only: no single trading day may exceed 50% of total profits when requesting a payout.
  • Daily Loss Limit on funded accounts: you may not lose more than 50% of your account's drawdown limit in a single trading day.
  • News Trading: exit all positions at least 2 minutes before major scheduled economic releases.
  • Maximum of 10 simultaneous funded or evaluation accounts per trader.
  • ONYX reserves the right to reduce account size, suspend, or terminate a Diamond account for rule violations.
  • Diamond accounts are simulated and do not represent entitlement to real capital unless explicitly stated.

Futures Trading Risk

Trading futures contracts involves substantial risk of loss and is not appropriate for all investors. Futures prices can be highly volatile and subject to rapid and unexpected changes due to a wide variety of factors including, but not limited to, government policy, political and economic conditions, weather, and other factors. Only risk capital — money you can afford to lose — should be used in any trading activity.

Leverage Risk

Futures contracts are leveraged instruments. This means a small price movement can result in a large gain or loss relative to the margin deposited. Leverage amplifies both profits and losses.
